[EDITOR'S DRAFT] Decentralized Identifiers Working Group Proposal

This is a proposal for the creation of a Decentralized Identifiers Working Group at W3C. This proposal has been put forward by a number of W3C member companies and community groups for examination by the greater W3C community. This work has been incubated for the past three years in the W3C Credentials Community Group, Rebooting the Web of Trust Design Salons, and Identity Workshops. The following documents have been prepared for the W3C Advisory Committee to evaluate the proposal.

  • Decentralized Identifiers Primer - An introduction to Decentralized Identifiers for W3C Advisory Committee Representatives. 3 minute read.
  • Decentralized Identifiers Working Group Draft Charter - The draft charter that is being proposed for approval by W3C membership. 15 minute read.

Supporting Documentation

  • Demonstration of Support for Decentralized Identifiers - Survey results proving that the Decentralized Identifiers work is widely supported by industry. 10 minute read.
  • Decentralized Identifiers FAQ - Frequently asked questions related to the Decentralized Identifiers work. 15 minute read.
  • Decentralized Identifiers Use Cases - A selection of Decentralized Identifiers use cases that are representative of the work. 30 minute read.
  • Proposed Decentralized Identifiers Data Model Specification - The input specification that the Decentralized Identifiers Working Group will attempt to standardize. 30 minute read.

About this Proposal

This proposal was created by the W3C Credentials Community Group (who initiated the W3C Verifiable Claims Working Group), the Rebooting the Web of Trust community, the Internet Identity Workshop community, and the Decentralized Identity Foundation.
